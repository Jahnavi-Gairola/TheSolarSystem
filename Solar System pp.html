SOLAR SYSTEM

The Solar System is the Sun and all the objects that orbit around it. The Sun is orbited by planets, asteroids, comets and other things.
The Solar System is about 4.6 billion years old. It formed by gravity in a large molecular cloud. Most of this matter gathered in the center, and the rest flattened into an orbiting disk that became the Solar System. It is thought that almost all stars form by this process.
The Sun is a star. It contains 99.9% of the Solar System's mass.This means that it has strong gravity. The other objects are pulled into orbit around the Sun. The Sun is mostly made out of hydrogen, and some helium. There are eight planets in the Solar System. Let's have a look on them...

MERCURY

FACTS ABOUT MERCURY
1. Mercury is one of the eight plantes of the solar system.
2. It is the smallest planet in our solar system.
3. Mercury does not have any moons or rings.
4. Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun.
5. Your weight on Mercury would be 38% of your weight on Earth.
6. A solar day on the surface of Mercury lasts 176 Earth days.
7. A year on Mercury takes 88 Earth days.
8. It has grey color.

It's been discovered that Mercury has no natural satellites. Mercury possesses a thin exosphere made up of atoms blasted off the surface by the solar wind and striking meteoroids. Mercury's exosphere is composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium and potassium. Therefore, we can't breathe in Mercury.No evidence for life has been found on Mercury.In reality, the average distance from the Sun to Mercury is roughly 58,000,000 km (35,000,000 miles).

FACTS ABOUT VENUS

5. Venus is the second brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon.
6. Venus has 90 times the atmospheric pressure of Earth.
7. Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty.
8. Venus was the first planet to have its motions plotted across the sky, as early as the second millennium BC.
9. It has yelowish brown color.


It's been discovered that Venus has no natural satellites.The atmosphere of Venus is made up mainly of carbon dioxide, and thick clouds of sulfuric acid completely cover the planet. The atmosphere traps the small amount of energy from the sun that does reach the surface along with the heat the planet itself releases. Life as we know it is impossible in Venus.The true 

                          average distance from the Sun to Venus is about 108,000,000 km (67,000,000 miles)

4. Saturn has only been visited 4 times by spacecraft.
5. TSaturn has 62 moons.
6. There could be life near Saturn.

Saturn's satellite system is very lopsided: one moon, Titan, comprises more than 96% of the mass in orbit around the planet. Saturn is approximately 75% hydrogen and 25% helium with traces of other substances like methane and water ice. From an average distance of 886 million miles (1.4 billion kilometers), Saturn is 9.5 astronomical units away from the Sun.

URANUS

FACTS ABOUT URANUS
1. Uranus is known as the “sideways planet” because it rotates on its side.
2. Uranus was discovered in 1781 by William Herschel .
3. Uranus was the first planet found using a telescope.
4. Uranus is an Ice Giant planet and nearly four times larger than Earth.
5. Uranus has 27 known moons , most of which are named after literary characters.
6. Like Saturn, Jupiter and Neptune, Uranus is a ringed planet.
7. It has blue green color.


Uranus has five major moons: Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, and Oberon. From an average distance of 1.8 billion miles (2.9 billion kilometers), Uranus is 19.8 astronomical units away from the Sun. One astronomical unit (abbreviated as AU), is the distance from the Sun to Earth. Uranus' atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, with a small amount of methane and traces of water and ammonia. The methane gives Uranus its signature blue color.

2. Neptune is the Smallest of the Gas Giants.
3. . Neptune’s Surface Gravity is Almost Earth-like.
4. The Discovery of Neptune is Still a Controversy.
5. Neptune has the Strongest Winds in the Solar System.
6. Neptune is the Coldest Planet in the Solar System.
7. Neptune Probably Captured its Largest Moon, Triton.
8. It has Dark blue color.


In order of distance from Neptune, the regular moons are Naiad, Thalassa, Despina, Galatea, Larissa, Hippocamp, and Proteus. Neptune's atmosphere is made up mostly of hydrogen and helium with just a little bit of methane. Neptune's neighbor Uranus is a blue-green color due to such atmospheric methane, but Neptune is a more vivid, brighter blue, so there must be an unknown component that causes the more intense color. From an average distance of 2.8 billion miles (4.5 billion kilometers), Neptune is 30 astronomical units away from the Sun. One astronomical unit (abbreviated as AU), is the distance from the Sun to Earth.
